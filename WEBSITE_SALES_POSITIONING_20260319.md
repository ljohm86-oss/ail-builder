## Current Positioning Truth

The current product is best positioned as:

- a CLI-first website generation and delivery system
- strongest on website-oriented frozen profiles
- suitable for repeatable generation, preview handoff, and managed sync

It is not yet best positioned as:

- a full application platform
- a dashboard generator
- a CMS platform
- a full ecommerce platform

## Best Current Sales Sentence

The safest short positioning today is:

AIL currently supports CLI-first generation and delivery for company and product websites, personal independent sites, minimal ecommerce storefronts, and after-sales service websites.

## Expanded Positioning

If more detail is needed, use:

AIL is currently strongest on website-oriented generation inside a stable frozen product surface. It can reliably produce and manage:

- company introduction and product websites
- personal independent websites
- minimal ecommerce independent storefronts
- after-sales service websites

It also supports blog-style personal websites in a narrower, partial sense.

## What We Can Say Confidently

These are safe claims today:

- website-oriented generation is the current strongest surface
- the current mainline is CLI-first
- supported outputs can be compiled, synced, previewed, and handed off through a stable workbench flow
- company/product website use cases are especially strong
- minimal storefront use cases are supported inside the `ecom_min` boundary
- after-sales website intake flows are supported

## What We Should Say Carefully

These are acceptable only with qualifiers:

### Blog-Style Sites

Safe phrasing:

- supports blog-style personal websites
- supports content-forward personal sites

Required qualifier:

- not a full blog platform
- not a CMS product

### Ecommerce

Safe phrasing:

- supports minimal ecommerce independent storefronts
- supports storefront-style list/detail/cart/checkout website flows

Required qualifier:

- not a full ecommerce platform
- not a merchant backend or operations system

### Enterprise or Brand Sites

Safe phrasing:

- supports enterprise or brand websites when the outcome is still marketing- or information-oriented

Required qualifier:

- not an internal enterprise portal
- not a business operations system

## What We Should Not Say

Avoid these claims:

- generates complete applications
- generates full dashboards
- supports full CMS workflows
- supports full ecommerce operations platforms
- supports admin systems or internal portals as current core product
- supports all website and app categories equally well

## Best-Fit Customer Language

The best current audience framing is:

- founders
- indie builders
- agencies doing website delivery
- teams needing fast product or brand websites
- teams needing minimal storefront or after-sales intake surfaces

This is stronger than trying to position the current product for:

- internal tool platforms
- business process systems
- enterprise software delivery
- complex SaaS product consoles

## Best-Fit Use Case Language

Safe use case wording:

- launch a product website quickly
- generate a personal or company website from a requirement
- generate a minimal storefront flow
- generate an after-sales intake website
- keep AIL as the source of truth while using preview and sync workflows

For a simple product narrative, use this order:

1. company and product websites
2. personal independent websites
3. minimal ecommerce storefronts
4. after-sales service websites
5. blog-style websites as a partial extension

This order matches the current strongest operational surface.

## Objection Handling

### “Can it build apps?”

Recommended answer:

Not as the current formal product surface. The strongest supported surface today is website-oriented generation, while app-style generation remains outside the current main promise.

### “Can it build a full ecommerce platform?”

Recommended answer:

It currently supports the storefront side well inside a minimal ecommerce boundary, but not the full merchant operations or platform layer.

### “Can it build a full blog or CMS?”

Recommended answer:

It can support blog-style personal websites, but not a full CMS or publishing platform as a formal promise today.

## One-Line Summary

The best current sales positioning is: AIL is a CLI-first system for generating and managing website-oriented outputs, strongest on company/product sites, personal independent sites, minimal storefronts, and after-sales service websites, while app, CMS, and full-platform promises remain out of scope.
