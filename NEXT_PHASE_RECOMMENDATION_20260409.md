## Why The Recommendation Changed

On 2026-04-03, the highest-leverage next step was still customization UX because:

- the workflow had just become stable
- the user-facing ergonomics were still too early to pause
- improving that path was the lowest-risk way to increase product value

That is no longer the best reading.

As of 2026-04-09:

- the customization surface is broad
- the customization docs are coherent enough
- the operator story is coherent enough
- the current validation anchor is strong enough
- the remaining customization work is mostly polish, not phase-defining work

That means the highest-value next step should move back to the user-visible product frontier.

## Recommended Next Phase

### Option A: Brand Distinction Phase

Current recommendation:

- **open this next**

### Core Question

Can AIL move from:

- strong, believable websites

to:

- more distinctive, recognisably authored, recognisably branded websites

without weakening the current stable supported surface?

### Why This Now Has The Highest Leverage

Because the current mainline no longer mainly needs safer editing mechanics.
It now needs stronger visible differentiation.

The clearest current gap is not:

- structure
- continuity
- runtime viability
- customization viability

The clearest current gap is:

- distinction
- stronger authored point of view
- stronger signature brand language
- less convergence toward safe but interchangeable output

### Safest First Cut

The safest first cut remains:

- continue on the company / product website line first

Why:

- it is the strongest mainstream proof line
- it has the most upside for visible differentiation without opening new route families
- improvements here would strengthen demos, sales positioning, and perceived product sharpness at once

## Updated Phase Order

The current best order is now:

1. **Brand Distinction Phase**
2. selective post-closeout customization polish
3. broader platform / scope expansion

## Why Broader Platform Is Still Not Next

The `Broader Platform Phase` still has the highest scope upside, but it is still not the safest next move.

Why not yet:

- the current supported scope is finally coherent
- the strongest next gain still comes from pushing quality and distinction within that scope
- opening broader platform work now would still risk dilution before the current surface has been fully leveraged

## What Success Would Look Like

If the next phase is Brand Distinction, success would mean:

1. personal sites feel more clearly authored and less like refined templates
2. company/product sites feel more clearly branded and less like strong but generic product-homepage systems
3. the strongest baselines become easier to distinguish from each other at first glance
4. AIL keeps the current structure and runtime stability while improving visible identity

## What This Recommendation Does Not Mean

This recommendation does **not** mean:

- customization no longer matters
- the customization surface is perfect
- ecommerce or after-sales should be ignored

It means only that:

- the next phase should be chosen by current leverage, not by inertia
- and current leverage now points more strongly at visible output distinction than at another long round of customization ergonomics
